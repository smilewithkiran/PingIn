📖 How to Use PingIN
Step-by-step guide based on your plan — follow in order for best results.
1
Download & Install the Extension 2 mins
Click the Download button above to get your plan's extension ZIP file. Then go to chrome://extensions in Chrome, enable Developer mode (top-right toggle), click Load unpacked, and select the extracted folder.
💡 Tip: Unzip the downloaded file first before loading it in Chrome. The folder should contain manifest.json inside.
2
Pin PingIN to Chrome Toolbar 30 secs
After installing, click the puzzle piece 🧩 icon in Chrome's top-right corner. Find PingIN in the list and click the pin icon 📌 next to it. This keeps PingIN always visible in your toolbar for one-click access.
3
Enter Your License Key 1 min
Open PingIN by clicking its icon. On first launch, enter the license key shown above in your dashboard. For Free Trial — just click the extension and your 5-day trial starts automatically, no key needed.
💡 Tip: Your license key was also emailed to you when you paid. Check your inbox if you can't find it here.
4
Open LinkedIn in the Same Chrome Window
Go to linkedin.com and make sure you're logged in to your LinkedIn account. PingIN works by reading LinkedIn pages — it needs LinkedIn open to collect profiles and run outreach.
💡 Tip: Use your regular LinkedIn account — PingIN sends connection requests as you, so make sure your profile is complete and professional.
5
You're Ready! Start with Smart Search ✅
Click the 🔍 Smart Search card in PingIN. Enter a job title (e.g. "Software Engineer"), select a company or college, pick a city, and click Search & Collect Profiles. LinkedIn will open and PingIN will automatically collect matching profiles.
1
Enter the Job Title / Keywords
Type what you're looking for — e.g. "Data Scientist", "React Developer", "Talent Acquisition Lead". Be specific for better results. Use quotes for exact matches.
💡 Tip: Use the ⚡ Boolean Builder button to build complex queries like "Data Scientist" AND (Python OR SQL) without typing manually.
2
Add Companies (Multi-chip input)
Type a company name and press Enter or comma to add it as a chip. Add multiple companies to search across all of them at once. Example: add Google, Amazon, Microsoft — PingIN searches all three.
3
Set Location & Experience Filters
Select a city (Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Mumbai, etc.) and experience level (0-1 yr, 1-3 yr, 3-5 yr, 5-10 yr, 10+ yr). Optionally add a college filter for IIT/NIT/IIM candidates.
💡 Tip: Leave company blank and search by college + role to find IIT/NIT alumni working anywhere in India — great for niche tech roles.
4
Click "Search & Collect Profiles"
LinkedIn opens with your search. PingIN automatically scrolls through results and collects profiles. When done, click View Collected Profiles → (the green button that appears) to see everyone found.
5
Save Searches with Search History 🕐
Click the 🕐 Search History button to save your current search. PingIN saves your last 15 searches — one click to re-run any previous search without re-entering all filters.
1
Collect Profiles First
Run a Smart Search and collect at least 10-20 profiles. All collected profiles appear in the Profiles tab. These are the people PingIN will reach out to on your behalf.
💡 Tip: Quality over quantity — 20 targeted profiles convert better than 50 random ones. Filter by experience and location before collecting.
2
Go to Outreach → Choose Campaign Type
From the Profiles screen, click Start Outreach. Choose your campaign type:
• Connect — sends connection requests only (safest, highest acceptance)
• Message — sends a message to existing 1st connections
• Visit — visits profiles to signal interest before connecting
3
Write Your Connection Note (Optional but Recommended)
Write a short personalised note (up to 300 chars). Keep it human, specific, not salesy. Example: "Hi [Name], saw your work at [Company] — we're hiring [Role] at [Your Company] and think your background is a great fit. Would love to connect!"
💡 Tip: Personalised notes get 3× more acceptance than blank requests. Mention something specific about their role or company.
4
Set Daily Limit — Keep it Safe Important
Set your daily limit to 20-50 requests. LinkedIn allows ~100/day but PingIN caps at 50 to keep your account safe. Don't send 50 every single day — vary between 20-40 to appear natural.
⚠️ Warning: Sending too many requests too fast can trigger LinkedIn restrictions. Start with 20/day for your first week, then increase gradually.
5
Monitor with Pause / Resume / Stop
During outreach, PingIN shows live progress. Use Pause if you need to stop temporarily (LinkedIn page loads slowly etc.), Resume to continue, or Stop to end the campaign. All sent profiles are tracked — PingIN never sends to the same profile twice.
🔒 These features require Pro or Annual plan.
1
🧬 AI Clone Search — Find 100 Similar Profiles
Go to Clone Search. Paste any LinkedIn profile URL of someone whose background you want to replicate. PingIN reads their headline, role, and education, then uses AI to find 100 similar professionals on LinkedIn.
💡 Best use case: You just made a great hire. Paste their LinkedIn URL — find 20 more exactly like them.
2
📄 JD Search — Paste Any Job Description
Click JD Search. Paste or upload any job description. PingIN's AI extracts the role, required skills, experience, and location — then builds the perfect LinkedIn search automatically. No need to manually translate JDs into search queries.
💡 Tip: Save frequently used JDs in the 📁 JD Library (save up to 10). One click to reuse any saved JD without repasting.
3
🎯 Predictive Sourcing — Find Passive Candidates
Click Predictive Sourcing. PingIN looks for candidates who show signals of being open to switching — recent activity patterns, profile views spike, recent company changes. These passive candidates are often the best hires because they're not being bombarded with offers.
4
📝 Candidate Notes — Never Forget Anyone
On any collected profile, click the 📝 note icon. Write private notes about the candidate — interview status, salary expectations, feedback. Notes are saved permanently and visible whenever you see that profile again.
💡 Pro tip: Use notes to track pipeline stages: "Messaged 15 Apr → Replied 16 Apr → Interview scheduled"
🔒 Developer Search requires Starter, Pro or Annual plan.
1
🐙 GitHub Multi-Skill Search — Find Real Developers
Go to Developer Search → GitHub tab. Enter 1 to 3 technical skills (e.g. React, Node.js, Python). PingIN searches GitHub for developers who actually use those skills in their real code — not just what they write on their CV.
💡 Why GitHub? A developer who commits React code every day is better evidence than "React" listed on LinkedIn. GitHub doesn't lie.
2
Use the PingIN GitHub Panel
When you open any GitHub profile, a PingIN panel slides in from the right. It shows the developer's bio, skills, and top languages. Click Find LinkedIn — PingIN scans their GitHub page for a LinkedIn link and opens their exact LinkedIn profile.
💡 Many developers put their LinkedIn link in their GitHub README or bio. PingIN finds it automatically — no manual searching.
3
Stack Overflow Top Experts
From Developer Search, switch to the Stack Overflow tab. Search for top contributors in a specific technology tag (e.g. "react", "python", "kubernetes"). These are your highest-quality leads — people who answer questions publicly are experts who keep learning.
4
Add to List → Connect via Smart Outreach
In the GitHub panel, click + Add to List for each developer you want to reach. Once you've added several, click Smart Outreach to run a campaign to connect with all of them on LinkedIn at once.
